How Schwab Portfolio Margin Works
PM vs Reg-T: Schwab Portfolio Margin applies OCC risk-based stress testing to your entire portfolio, not position-by-position Reg-T rules. For broad-based index options (SPX, RUT, NDX), the margin requirement equals the maximum possible loss across 10 stress scenarios (SPX ±6%, ±9%, ±12%, ±15% and two volatility shifts). For defined-risk spreads, this always equals the spread width minus credit received.
SPX-specific advantage: SPX options are cash-settled European-style — no early assignment risk. PM treats the long leg as full collateral for the short leg within the same expiration. For diagonals, the long back-month is credited as collateral; PM requirement ≈ net debit paid.

Per-Strategy BP Formulas
Bull Put Spread & Bull Put Spread HV Credit spread
BP = (short_strike − long_strike − credit_received) × $100
= (spread_width − premium) × $100
Typical example — NORMAL regime, SPX ≈ 5800, VIX ≈ 18:
Short put δ0.30 → strike ≈ 5,640  |  Long put δ0.15 → strike ≈ 5,500
Spread width = 140 pts  |  Credit received ≈ $35/pt = $3,500/contract
BP per contract = (140 − 35) × $100 = $10,500

With risk budget $3,000: contracts = 3,000 ÷ 3,500 = 0.86 → BP used ≈ $9,000 = 6.0% of account

HV variant (δ0.20, DTE=35, half-size): spread ≈ 200 pts, credit ≈ $32 → BP/c ≈ $16,800 — but half-size halves the risk budget, so BP% ≈ 5%.
Iron Condor Credit · 2-sided
BP = (max(call_spread_width, put_spread_width) − total_net_credit) × $100
Key insight: Both sides cannot simultaneously be at max loss — PM uses only the wider side's max loss, reduced by the full net credit (both call spread + put spread credit combined).

Typical example — LOW_VOL regime, SPX ≈ 5800, IVP 25–45:
Wing width ≈ 1.5% × 5800 = 87 pts (rounded to $100)
Short call δ0.16 / Short put δ0.16 · 45 DTE
Net credit ≈ $15/pt total = $1,500/contract
BP per contract = (100 − 15) × $100 = $8,500

BP% ≈ $3,000 ÷ $1,500 × $8,500 ÷ $150,000 = ~11.3% — IC is the most capital-intensive strategy in this account.

IVP filter (20–50): <20 → insufficient premium; >50 → tail risk exceeds credit.
Bull Call Diagonal Debit · directional
BP = net_debit_paid × $100
= (long_call_price − short_call_price) × $100
Structure: Long 90-DTE call at δ0.70 (deep ITM) + Short 30-DTE call at δ0.30 (OTM).
The long ITM call provides full collateral. PM treats this like a covered call: worst-case loss = net debit paid if both expire worthless.

Typical example — LOW_VOL regime, SPX ≈ 5800:
Long call (90 DTE, δ0.70, strike ≈ 5600) ≈ $215/pt = $21,500
Short call (30 DTE, δ0.30, strike ≈ 5900) ≈ $20/pt = $2,000
Net debit = $195/pt = $19,500/contract
BP per contract = $19,500

With $3,000 risk budget: contracts = 3,000 ÷ 19,500 = 0.154 → BP used = $3,000 = 2.0% exactly
BP% always equals risk_pct — because BP = premium = risk budget.

PM Risk Notes & Watchpoints
Margin call risk: If account equity drops below $100,000, Schwab converts the account from PM to Reg-T automatically. Reg-T margins are 3–5× higher for the same positions and can trigger forced liquidation. Maintain a buffer above $125,000.

BP Optimization Research Agenda
Current state: Average BP utilization is only 2.8% — the account is effectively idle 97%+ of the time. The key research question is whether we can improve capital efficiency while controlling for drawdown risk.
# Research Question Hypothesis
1 Increase risk_pct from 2% → 3–4%? Scales PnL linearly; also scales drawdown linearly. Sharpe unchanged. Max single-trade BP stays ≤ 40% (IC at 4% risk_pct ≈ 14.4%).
2 Size by BP% (not premium%) — target fixed BP% per trade? BPS gets fewer contracts (large BP footprint) vs diagonal (tiny BP). Reduces concentration in high-BP strategies; may hurt PnL on diagonals which dominate the win-rate.
3 Cap on Iron Condor BP relative to account? IC uses 7–12% BP with low absolute PnL contribution. Explicit cap at 8% may require reducing wing width or passing on some IC entries.
4 Allow 2 simultaneous positions (split budget)? While diagonal is open (2% BP), a BPS can coexist (6% BP) → combined 8% BP. Requires overlapping-position logic in backtest engine. Significant code change; needs careful drawdown analysis.
