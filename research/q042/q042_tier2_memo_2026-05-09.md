## Verdict

**Tier 2 PASS — recommend DRAFT Spec.** Findings:

1. **Best entry config:** `dd60 ≥ 12% from 60d high + close > MA50 reclaim within 30 trading days`
2. **Best structure:** `ATM/+5% call spread, DTE 30`
3. **BP-stacking fear was overstated** — main strategy operates in low-BP mode (mean 2.8% at HIGH_VOL triggers in 2009-2026 era) so the proposed default gate `min(20%, max(0, 60% − main_bp%))` essentially never fires; default gate is **redundant** by current main-strategy parameters but worth keeping as governance backstop.

Estimated capital efficiency: **~+$3.5 / $100 BP / day = ~7× V_A baseline** at the winner config (n=41 trades over 19 years, 73% win rate).

## P2 — Structure grid (BS + skew haircut)

### Pricing assumptions

- BS with σ = max(VIX/100, 10% floor)
- **Term-structure multiplier** on σ:
  - DTE ≤ 45: σ × 1.10 (short-dated noisier)
  - DTE 46-120: σ × 1.00
  - DTE > 120: σ × 0.95
- **Skew multiplier** on σ (linear in moneyness K/S):
  - ATM: 1.00
  - K = 1.05 × S: 0.93 (call skew haircut)
  - K = 1.10 × S: 0.85
  - K = 0.95 × S: 1.07 (put skew premium)
  - K = 0.90 × S: 1.15
- r = 4%, hold to expiry
- BP = full premium for long structures; for ratio 1×2 BP ≈ 20% × 2S (PM naked margin proxy — see caveat)

### Top-5 structures by `$ / $100 BP / day` (median, full 2007-2026 sample)

| Trigger | Structure | n | median $/$100BP/day | win% | full-period median % | worst % |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **dd12 + ma50_reclaim** | **spread ATM/+5% DTE 30** | 41 | **+$3.525** | 73.2% | +$105.7 | -$100 |
| dd12 + ma50_reclaim | spread ATM/+3% DTE 30 | 41 | +$3.262 | 75.6% | +$97.9 | -$100 |
| dd10 + ma50_reclaim | spread ATM/+3% DTE 30 | 56 | +$3.281 | 71.4% | +$98.4 | -$100 |
| dd15 naive | spread ATM/+5% DTE 30 | 192 | +$2.772 | 59.4% | +$83.2 | -$100 |
| dd10 + ma50_reclaim | spread ATM/+5% DTE 30 | 56 | +$2.599 | 69.6% | +$78.0 | -$100 |

**vs V_A baseline `$0.485 / $100 BP-day` ($4.85 / $1000 BP-day)** — winner is **~7.3× baseline**.

### Why short DTE wins

- BP-day denominator scales with DTE → short DTE has 3-4× lower denominator
- ATM/+5% strikes realize close to max gain at the median 30-day forward return at dd12 entry (+12%)
- Vol crush from VIX-elevated entry to mean-reverting environment helps the long ATM call (positive vega bleed offset by direction)

### Non-spread structures (FYI — all weaker than spread DTE 30)

| Structure | Best $/$100BP/day | Note |
|---|---:|---|
| LEAP ATM (DTE 365) | +$0.42 (dd12+ma50) | 90% win rate but BP-day denom too large |
| LEAP Δ0.30 | +$0.12 (dd12+ma50) | OTM strike + skew haircut + IV mean reversion = barely positive |
| Long ATM call DTE 120 | +$0.79 (dd12+ma50) | Highest single-leg, but 0.49 win rate (path-dep) |
| Ratio 1×2 (long ATM / short 2× +5%) | +$0.18 (best case) | Worst-case truncated to ~-$30 (vs -$100 for spread), but BP proxy uncertain — undefined upside risk in PM margin during VIX shock |

### Structure caveats

- **No transaction costs** in numbers above. SPX/SPXW spread cost ≈ $0.20-0.50 per leg → for ATM/+5% DTE 30 spread (~$25 net debit), tx cost adds 1.6-4% drag per trade. Materially shrinks the +$3.5 lead but doesn't reverse it.
- **No early-close optimization.** Holding to expiry on losing trades = -100%. Spec-level rule could add a "close at 50% loss" stop that would lift win rate but compress avg gain.
- **Skew haircut is approximate.** Real SPX skew is regime-dependent (steeper in HIGH_VOL — exactly when Q042 enters). My linear haircut likely **understates** the skew premium during entry; ATM call entry IV is probably higher than VIX × 1.0 in real conditions, which would compress the spread debit further → real $/BP-day likely better than +$3.5 in HIGH_VOL entries (skew works in long-call's favor on a relative basis when entered during peak vol).
- **Ratio 1×2 BP proxy is fragile.** Real PM naked-call margin during VIX>30 ≠ 20% notional; could be 30-40% notional per short. If your account has SPAN/PM and live margin shows much higher, ratio becomes uneconomic. **Tier 3 / Spec drafting must use live margin engine numbers**, not Tier 2 proxy.

## P3 — BP-stacking gate simulation (Q3 revisited)

### Tier 1 Q3 concern (now revised)

Tier 1 framing: "Q042 stacks BP exactly when main strategy is stressed (98.5% HIGH_VOL overlap) → BP collision risk is severe."

**This was directionally wrong.** Main strategy by design **reduces gross during HIGH_VOL** (BPS_HV / IC_HV are smaller-BP variants). The 98% regime overlap is real, but the BP usage in those regimes is *low*, not high.

### 19y BP envelope (full main-strategy backtest 2007-01 → 2026-05)

| Metric | All days | HIGH_VOL only | LOW_VOL only |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Mean bp_pct | 6.3% | 9.3% | 5.7% |
| Median bp_pct | 4.3% | 4.7% | 4.8% |
| p95 bp_pct | 23.6% | — | — |
| Max bp_pct | 53.2% (2007-08) | 53.2% | 22.8% |

**By era**: 2007-2008 had elevated BP (mean 15-20%, max 53%); 2009-2016 moderate (5-13%); 2017-2026 very low (1-4% mean). Strategy parameter evolution explains the regime shift.

### Main strategy BP at Q042 trigger dates

| Trigger | n | main_bp_pct median | p75 | max |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| dd10 + ma50_reclaim | 56 | 2.8% | 3.5% | 34.3% |
| dd12 + ma50_reclaim | 41 | 2.8% | 2.8% | 31.1% |
| dd15 naive | 192 | 0.0% | 3.6% | 36.5% |

Main strategy is essentially flat or low-gross at trigger time across all 19 years.

### Gate firing rates (proposed default: `min(20%, max(0, 60% − main_bp%))`)

| Trigger | Gate | blocked | partial | full | combined peak BP |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| dd12 + ma50_reclaim | default (max=20%, total=60%) | 0/41 | 0 | 41 | 51.1% |
| dd12 + ma50_reclaim | conservative (max=15%, total=50%) | 0/41 | 0 | 41 | 46.1% |
| dd12 + ma50_reclaim | aggressive (max=25%, total=70%) | 0/41 | 0 | 41 | 56.1% |
| dd15 naive | default | 0/192 | 0 | 192 | 56.5% |

**Gate fire rate = 0% across all 19 years on all gate variants.** With the current main-strategy parameter set, the gate is functionally inert — Q042 always gets full allocation.

### Hold-period collision (winner config: dd12+ma50_reclaim + spread DTE 30)

Worst-case Q042 BP held constant 30 days against varying main BP:

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Combined peak BP during hold (median) | 22.8% |
| Combined peak BP during hold (p75) | 35.6% |
| Combined peak BP during hold (max) | **67.2%** |
| Days where combined > 80% account | 0 / 41 |
| Days where combined > 100% account | 0 / 41 |

### Gate recommendation

**Keep the default gate as governance backstop, not as primary control.** Rationale:

1. Gate is currently inert but **regime-conditional on main-strategy parameters**. If main strategy migrates to V_D / V_J (Q021 sizing-up variants) the BP envelope shifts and gate becomes load-bearing.
2. Gate cost is zero (no false positives in current era) — keeping it costs nothing.
3. The 67% combined peak is well within PM margin headroom (which the codebase considers normal up to ~80% before warning), so no live-account risk.
4. **Q042-specific cap**: cap Q042 at 20% account regardless of headroom. This is the binding constraint, not the joint cap.

## Recommended Tier 3 / DRAFT Spec parameters

```yaml
strategy: q042_directional_overlay
trigger:
  drawdown_threshold: 0.12       # dd60 ≥ 12% from 60-day high
  confirmation: ma50_reclaim     # close > MA50 within 30 trading days post-trigger
  reset_after_re_high: true      # re-arm trigger when SPX revisits 60d high

structure:
  type: call_spread
  dte_target: 30
  long_leg: ATM
  short_leg: ATM + 5% (strike rounded to nearest $5 SPX or $1 XSP)

position_sizing:
  bp_per_entry_pct_account: 1.0  # 1% account per entry (start small)
  q042_account_cap_pct: 20.0     # absolute cap regardless of headroom
  joint_gate:                    # governance backstop, currently inert
    formula: min(q042_cap, max(0, total_cap − main_bp%))
    q042_cap: 20.0
    total_cap: 60.0

exit:
  primary: held_to_expiry        # MVP — Tier 4 should test 50% take-profit / 50% stop
  early_close_optional: false    # defer to Spec drafting
```

## Tier 3 / Spec drafting unknowns

Items that **must** be resolved before SPEC drafting:

1. **Live SPX option chain pricing** — replace BS+skew with broker-API midpoint at signal time. Confirms or refutes the +$3.5 / $100 BP-day economics under realistic IV / skew / spread.
2. **Ratio 1×2 PM margin reality check** — pull live PM margin numbers for naked-style structures during VIX>30; decide if ratio is a legitimate Tier-2 alternative or strictly inferior.
3. **Re-trigger spacing** — current trigger logic re-fires whenever a new dd60 trough occurs. Need an explicit spacing rule (min N days between Q042 entries) to prevent doubling up during a long drawdown.
4. **Exit rules MVP test** — held-to-expiry vs 50% take-profit vs trail; test on the n=41 winner sample with realistic intraday data.
5. **Symbol selection** — SPX (1×100 multiplier, $5 strike granularity) vs XSP (1×100 mini, $1 strike granularity). At small position size, XSP allows more granular entry.
6. **Account scale gate** — at NLV ≥ $X, this strategy becomes economic. Below $X (e.g., NLV < $50k), the spread's $25 debit + 1% account cap means 0 contracts — the strategy doesn't activate. Define the threshold.

Items that are **NOT** blockers (can be Tier 4):

- Optimal stop-loss methodology (early Tier 3 default = held to expiry)
- Multi-leg variants (calendar, diagonal, butterfly)
- VIX-conditional position sizing
- Rolling rules

## Caveats and unknowns (Tier 2 limitations)

- **No live IV surface data** in repo (only `data/q041_chains/` 2 days of single-stocks). All P2 numbers are BS + linear-skew approximation. Direction of bias is **mixed**: skew haircut likely understates real skew premium in HIGH_VOL → understates spread profitability; flat-vol assumption in path → overstates win rate (no IV path drag).
- **No transaction cost in P2 numbers.** Estimated 1.6-4% per-trade haircut for SPX; XSP slightly worse due to wider relative spreads.
- **19y baseline backtest uses current main-strategy parameter set.** If Q021 V_D / V_J / V_G ever promote, BP envelope shifts upward and gate becomes load-bearing — re-run P3 at that point.
- **Sample sizes**: dd12+ma50_reclaim has n=41 over 19 years. Statistical confidence on win rate (73%) is medium — 95% CI roughly ±10pp. Tier 3 should validate on out-of-sample post-spec data.
- **dd20 falling-knife pattern** dropped from finalist due to 3m path risk. Tier 3 could revisit with a "dd20 + 60-day delay" trigger to catch the bottom-out without the early drawdown.
- **The Tier 1 Q3 concern was wrong on direction.** Main strategy de-grosses in HIGH_VOL by design; Q042 is functionally orthogonal in BP space at present. Logged here as a methodology lesson — regime overlap ≠ BP collision.
