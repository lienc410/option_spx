## Q1. Drawdown depth → forward SPX return

**Answer (one line):** Drawdown ≥10% from a 60-day high produces a clearly positive-skewed forward distribution (12-mo median **+21%** at dd10 / **+30%** at dd15, 12-mo positive rate **82-100%**) versus an unconditional 12-mo median of **+13%** with positive rate 79% — the edge is real but starts at dd10, not dd5.

| Trigger | n | 3m median | 6m median | 12m median | 12m positive% |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| dd60 ≥ 5% | 1,153 | +5.0% | +7.9% | +17.4% | 76.8% |
| dd60 ≥ 10% | 480 | +5.2% | +11.7% | +21.2% | 81.7% |
| dd60 ≥ 15% | 192 | +8.2% | +15.9% | +29.8% | **97.9%** |
| dd60 ≥ 20% | 88 | -7.2% | +6.0% | +27.6% | **100.0%** |
| **unconditional baseline** | 4,616 | +3.6% | +6.3% | +12.7% | 78.8% |

**Falling-knife observation at dd20:** 3-mo median is **-7.2%** with only 36% positive rate — entries during the worst phase of a draw-down are early. By 12 months they fully recover (100% positive), but the path matters for premium decay.

### MA50 reclaim filter (entered only on first day after trigger that close > MA50, within 30-day window)

|---|---:|---:|---:|
| dd60 ≥ 5% | 196 (-83%) | 69.7% (was 67.9%) | 86.5% (was 76.8%) |
| dd60 ≥ 10% | 56 (-88%) | **75.0%** (was 68.8%) | **92.9%** (was 81.7%) |

| dd60 ≥ 20% | 6 (-93%) | 33.3% (was 36.4%) | 100.0% (was 100.0%) |

**Reclaim filter is real but expensive:** at dd10 it lifts 3-mo win rate by ~6pp and 12-mo win rate by ~11pp, but cuts sample by ~88%. **Not a free lunch** — Tier 2 should test reclaim as one of several entry confirmations alongside breadth and vol-normalization candidates, weighted by the cost of waiting (most upside materialized by 6m).

## Q2. Option structure economics (10% dd60 trigger sample, n=480)

**Answer (one line):** ATM/+5% **call spread (DTE 90)** is the only structure whose median payoff clears the main-strategy BP-day baseline (median **+$1.49 / $100 BP / day ≈ $14.9 / $1000 BP / day** vs V_A baseline **$4.85 / $1000 BP / day**); the LEAP ATM call is positive but **below baseline**; the LEAP Δ0.35 call is **structurally negative** (median PnL = -100% of premium, win rate 19%).

### Pricing assumptions (Tier 1 simplification)

- BS pricing with σ = max(VIX_at_entry/100, 10% floor), r = 4%
- LEAP: DTE=365, hold to expiry, payoff = max(0, S₂₅₂ − K) − premium
- Call spread: DTE=90, hold to expiry, payoff = max(0, S₆₃ − K_long) − max(0, S₆₃ − K_short) − net debit
- BP = full premium (long premium account)
- **Caveat: no IV path / no skew / no transaction costs / no early-close optimization.** Tier 2 must re-price with a vol-surface model or historical IV.

### Results — full-period payoff per $100 BP

| Structure | median | avg | win% | worst | best |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| LEAP ATM (DTE 365, K=S) | **+$32.3** | +$29.0 | 66.5% | -$100 | +$231 |
| LEAP Δ0.35 (DTE 365, K>S) | -$100.0 | -$58.1 | 19.2% | -$100 | +$293 |
| Call spread (ATM/+5%, DTE 90) | **+$133.7** | +$44.8 | 63.7% | -$100 | +$149 |

### Results — $/BP-day normalized (per $100 BP per day)

| Structure | median $/BP-day | vs V_A baseline ($0.485 / $100 BP-day) |
|---|---:|---|
| LEAP ATM | +$0.088 | ❌ **18%** of baseline |
| LEAP Δ0.35 | -$0.274 | ❌ **negative** |
| Call spread (ATM/+5%, 90d) | **+$1.486** | ✅ **3.1× baseline** |

**Why the spread wins despite lower max upside:** capped payoff (max gain ≈ +50% on debit) doesn't matter for $/BP-day because (a) shorter holding period (90d vs 365d) shrinks the BP-day denominator 4×, (b) median forward 90d return at dd10 is +5.2% which is exactly where ATM/+5% spreads expire near max, and (c) the OTM short leg meaningfully reduces premium.

**Why LEAP Δ0.35 is structurally negative:** OTM strike + median 12-mo SPX return at dd10 = +21% which often **isn't enough** to push a Δ0.35 strike into the money against IV-paid-up entry premium. Drawdowns coincide with VIX spikes (Q3 below) → premium paid at peak vol, vol mean-reverts, OTM strike never realized → expires worthless 81% of trials.

## Q3. Regime overlap with main strategy (HIGH_VOL = VIX>22)

**Answer (one line):** Q042 trigger dates are **almost entirely inside HIGH_VOL** — at dd10 trigger **98.5%** of entries occur with VIX>22 (vs unconditional baseline 27.9%); this means Q042 BP demand stacks on top of the main strategy's most-stressed regime, making **explicit BP-stacking governance mandatory at Tier 2.**

| Trigger | n | VIX median | VIX p75 | HIGH_VOL rate |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| dd60 ≥ 5% | 1,153 | 26.1 | 32.7 | 77.6% |
| dd60 ≥ 10% | 480 | 32.9 | 44.2 | **98.5%** |
| dd60 ≥ 15% | 192 | 46.0 | 56.8 | 100.0% |
| dd60 ≥ 20% | 88 | 58.0 | 66.3 | 100.0% |
| **unconditional baseline** | 4,868 | — | — | 27.9% |

**Implication:**
- Q042 is **not orthogonal** to main strategy by design — it activates exactly when main strategy is in BPS_HV / IC_HV reduced posture.
- Stacking risk is asymmetric: Q042 is **long premium** (positive vega) while main strategy is **short premium** (negative vega). On the surface they hedge each other on vega; on BP they compete for the same headroom.
- Vega offset is real but **incomplete**: Q042 long-premium expression captures upside convexity (positive delta-gamma in rallies); main strategy short-premium captures vol crush (positive theta-vega in mean reversion). They converge in a sustained-grind-down regime where neither wins (the IV-expansion failure mode flagged in Q019 affects main strategy; Q042 survives via positive delta but loses theta).

**Tier 2 must answer (gating questions):**
1. What is the BP cap for Q042 entries when main strategy is at >X% BP? (capacity gate)
2. Does Q042 vega-long offset reduce the main strategy's IV-expansion stress (the Q019 worst-year scenario)? Quantify.
3. Should Q042 be funded out of overlay headroom (treated as overlay) or out of dedicated BP slice (treated as separate sleeve)? — this is a structural choice that affects sizing math.

## Tier 2 promotion recommendation

Promote with the following scope:

1. **Trigger calibration** — refine dd10 vs dd15 vs dd15+reclaim vs other technical confirmations (200dma, breadth, VIX-term-structure-based). Use the same dataset and forward distributions but evaluate via $/BP-day net of realistic option re-pricing.
2. **Structure refinement** — the call spread is the Tier 1 winner; Tier 2 should:
   - Test 30/60/90/120 DTE × ATM/+5%/+10%/Δ-targets grid
   - Add ratio spread (1× long ATM / 2× short OTM) and risk reversal as defensive variants
   - Re-price with a proper IV-surface model (skew + term structure) — VIX-as-σ overstates spread debit (skew makes downside puts richer, ATM calls cheaper than VIX implies), so my Tier 1 spread numbers are likely **conservative**.
3. **Regime gating** — explicit BP-stacking gate (Q3 above). Default proposal to test: Q042 BP cap = `min(20% account, max(0, 60% account − main_strategy_BP%))`.
4. **Out-of-sample check** — split 2007-2018 vs 2019-2026 (the latter contains the post-COVID structurally-different vol regime); validate edge holds on the second half.

## Caveats and unknowns

- **No transaction costs / slippage** in Tier 1 numbers. SPX/SPXW spreads ≈ $0.20-0.50 per leg; Tier 2 should haircut.
- **No IV-surface skew** — using flat VIX as σ underprices the long ATM call and overprices the OTM legs. Direction of bias on the spread is **conservative**.
- **No BP scaling to actual SPX/XSP contract size** — Tier 1 is normalized "per $100 BP". Live execution requires SPEC-072-style discrete-contract re-grossing.
- **`$4.85 / BP-day` baseline unit interpretation** — codebase computes `pnl_per_bp_day = total_pnl / sum(bp_used)` where bp_used appears to be in dollars but yields V_A=$4.85 for ~3-yr V_A backtest with bp_days=83,201, implying bp_days are most likely **in $1000-day units**. If that interpretation is wrong, the comparison ratios above shift but the **rank order LEAP_Δ35 < LEAP_ATM < spread** is robust.
- **Survivorship: dd20 sample n=88 is small** — confidence interval on its 3-mo median (−7.2%) is wide. Tier 2 should bootstrap the worst-bucket distribution before sizing.
- **2007-2026 contains 3 distinct vol regimes** (GFC, post-2010 grind, COVID era) — pooled distribution may mask regime-conditional behavior. Tier 2 split required.
