The first dd15 trigger per cluster lands near the trough — effectively a "deeper drawdown anchor".

## A1 — DTE Path-Tolerance Multi-Metric

ATM/+5% call spread, full sample (no overlap filter), T_close entry baseline:

| Trigger | DTE | n | Win rate | Median PnL/$100BP | $/BP-day | Max consec losses | Hit short strike% | Annualized account% (1% sizing) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **dd12_ma50_reclaim** | **30** | 41 | 73% | +105.7 | $3.52 | 6 | 51% | +120.7%/yr |
| **dd12_ma50_reclaim** | **60** | 41 | 73% | +92.5 | $1.54 | 7 | 59% | +103.4%/yr |
| **dd12_ma50_reclaim** | **90** | 41 | **80%** | +83.4 | $0.97 | 6 | **73%** | +125.3%/yr |
| dd12_ma50_reclaim | 120 | 41 | 78% | +77.3 | $0.67 | 5 | 71% | +104.8%/yr |
| dd15_naive | 30 | 192 | 59% | +83.2 | $2.77 | 29 | 51% | +247.5%/yr* |
| dd15_naive | 60 | 192 | 59% | +78.7 | $1.31 | 37 | 57% | +166.1%/yr* |
| dd15_naive | 90 | 192 | 60% | +69.9 | $0.78 | 35 | 55% | +117.9%/yr* |

\* dd15 naive unfiltered numbers are misleading — see A3 for sequence-loss collapse.

**Key insight**: DTE 90 is the right MVP DTE for dd12+reclaim. Higher win rate (80% vs 73%), more trades hit the recovery target (73% vs 51%), and the higher absolute PnL on max-gain trades compensates the lower $/BP-day denominator.

**Delayed-recovery rescue**: of the 11 DTE 30 losers in dd12+reclaim, DTE 90 rescues only 5/11 (45%) — meaning when DTE 30 loses, longer DTE often still loses (structural failure, not pure timing). DTE 90's edge over DTE 30 is from **better entry capture**, not from rescuing duds.

## A2 — Execution Timing Sensitivity

T+0 close vs T+1 open vs T+1 close vs T+2 close, ATM/+5% spread, dd12+reclaim trigger:

| DTE | Variant | Median PnL/$100BP | Δ vs T_close | Win rate | Drift in S |
|---:|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 30 | T_close | +105.7 | (baseline) | 73% | 0% |
| 30 | T+1 open | +100.5 | **−5.2** | 76% | −0.02% |
| 30 | T+1 close | +86.5 | **−19.2** | 76% | +0.23% |
| 30 | T+2 close | +90.8 | −14.9 | 76% | +0.20% |
| 90 | T_close | +87.4 | (baseline) | 80% | 0% |
| 90 | T+1 open | +83.4 | **−4.0** | 80% | −0.02% |

**Recommended execution: T+1 open** (alert generated at T close, executed at next-day open). Realistic, captures most of T+0 economics, supported by DTE 90.

## A4 — Re-Trigger Spacing Rule

Gap distribution for dd12+reclaim raw triggers (n=41):
- median 3 days, p25 1 day, p75 106 days, max 1599 days
- 28/40 gaps are < 30 days (mostly 2008-2009 GFC clustering)

**Recommended rule**: max 1 active Q042 spread at any time (no-overlap = min-spacing = DTE).

For DTE 90, this means after entering, no new Q042 entry until expiry (90 days later). Equivalent rules:
- "min 90 days between entries"
- "Q042 sleeve == 1 position max"
- "skip new triggers while a Q042 position is held"

**Effect on production sample**: dd12+reclaim 41 raw → 13 filtered (-68%); dd15 naive 192 raw → 11 filtered (-94%). Both collapse to ~0.6-0.8 trades/year.

## A5 — SPX vs XSP Economics

| Attribute | SPX | XSP |
|---|---|---|
| Index level (latest) | ~7,400 | ~740 |
| Multiplier | 100 | 100 |
| Strike granularity | $5 | $1 |
| ATM/+5% spread debit (VIX 25, DTE 90) | ~$11,098 | ~$1,110 |
| Per-leg bid-ask | $0.20-0.50 | $0.05-0.15 |
| Per-spread tx cost | $40-100 | $10-30 |
| Tx cost as % of debit | 0.4-0.9% | 0.9-2.7% |
| Tax | Section 1256 (60/40) | Section 1256 (60/40) |
| Settlement | Cash-settled European | Cash-settled European |

**Trade-off**: SPX has lower tx-cost-as-%-debit at scale; XSP has 10× lower min position size. For account NLV $100k-$1M, XSP is the only viable choice.

## Updated Failure-Mode Catalog

(incorporating reviewer's FM1 / FM2 additions)

### FM1 — Vol crush + delayed recovery (reviewer's addition)
> Trigger fires during elevated IV; SPX does not fall further but recovery is slow; IV crush + theta decay kills the spread before directional move arrives.

**Mitigation**: DTE 90 (vs DTE 30) provides 60 extra days for the directional thesis to play out, and benefits from IV mean reversion via the long ATM call. A2 confirms DTE 30 is uniquely vulnerable to this mode.

### FM2 — Sequence bleed in secular bear / prolonged chop (reviewer's addition)
> Multiple dd12+reclaim triggers in a multi-leg bear market; each spread loses 1% (capped); cumulative bleed is the real risk.

**Mitigation**: A3b sequence metrics + no-overlap rule. Filtered metrics show max 3 consecutive losses for dd12+reclaim DTE 90, max 2 for dd15+naive DTE 90. At 1% sizing, max consec loss is 2-3% account. Worst 12m is -2.2% (dd12+reclaim) or -1.2% (dd15 naive). Acceptable within 5% sleeve cap.

### Original failure modes still apply
- Trigger fires, SPX continues down → max loss = full debit (1% account at 1% sizing)
- Trigger fires, sideways grinding → theta decay (FM1 adjacent)
- Trigger fires, SPX rallies past +5% short strike → capped at max gain
- 1929-style depression / pre-2007 regimes → untested (0 historical samples)

### Excluded from concern
- Naked-margin cascade: N/A (defined-risk debit spread)
- Dividend assignment: N/A (SPX/XSP cash-settled European)
- Margin call: only via debit cost; bounded
- Gap risk against long leg: bounded by spread structure
