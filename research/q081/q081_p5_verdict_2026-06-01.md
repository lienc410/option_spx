## Verdict 2 — NEW SPEC: debit-strategy cash-budget cap

**Add a `cash_budget_pct` cap** at the sleeve governance layer (above
SPEC-104's BP cap), governing aggregate debit-strategy footprint as a
fraction of combined liquid cash.

### Specification

- **Cap**: `Σ debit_open ≤ 0.65 × combined_liquid_cash`
- **Denominator**: combined liquid cash across all brokers (Schwab raw cash
  + Schwab cash-like + E-Trade raw cash). Read at open-decision time.
- **Numerator**: aggregate of entry debit ($USD) for all currently-open
  debit-strategy positions (BCD; future debit strategies inherit cap).
- **Behavior on violation**: new debit-strategy open blocked, fall back to
  reduce_wait (or selector's existing degraded routing).
- **Cap floor**: even at minimum sizing, single BCD must fit within cap.
  If liquid cash is < $30k, BCD opens are auto-blocked.

### Calibration: why 65%?

Single typical BCD debit = $24k = 65% of $37k baseline → cap allows 1 BCD
at current backtest-validated sizing.

| Cap level | 1 BCD fits? | 2 concurrent? | Slack preserved |
|---|---|---|---|
| 50% liquid | ❌ (must shrink BCD) | ❌ | $18.5k |
| **65% liquid (proposed)** | ✅ | ❌ | $13k |
| 80% liquid | ✅ | ❌ | $7.5k |
| 100% liquid | ✅ | ❌ | $0 |

65% chosen because:
- Allows current backtest-validated sizing (no need to change BCD ladder)
- Hard-blocks accidental 2-BCD concurrent open (which P1 showed never
  happened but is the forward risk)
- Preserves $13k operational slack for: dividend cycles, unexpected margin
  calls on stock positions, spontaneous BPS opportunities (which DO need
  cash for collateral edge cases)

### Why this is the main Q081 actionable

Per G-review 1: with crowd-out judged non-issue and routing qualitatively
ratified, sizing/cap is the **only Q081 output that lands a real
governance change**. Defering it would close Q081 with no actionable
("matrix unchanged" + "routing OK" + "no SPEC" = research closed
unproductively, exactly the "close-too-cleanly" trap 2nd quant flagged).

The cap is also the future-proof prophylactic: P1's 0 crowd-out came from
sequential ladder discipline, but nothing in current code enforces that
discipline. If matrix evolves to allow concurrent debit positions, the
cap is what prevents cash-displacement of QQQ.

## Verdict 3 — Documented but not acted on

### Forward risks
1. **Cash-bound profile could shift**: PM may rebalance differently in
   the future. Verdict assumes current profile (3% liquid, 60% beta).
   Cap is in % terms so adapts automatically, but the appropriateness of
   65% calibration would need re-audit if profile changes >20%.
2. **Single-trade worst-case = 8.8% of baseline cash**: not catastrophic
   but real. PM should be aware that one bad BCD eats ~9% of operational
   cash even without breaching cap.
3. **Sequential ladder is a code-implicit invariant**: nothing currently
   enforces "only one BCD open at a time" beyond selector routing rules.
   Cap (Verdict 2) plus existing one-strategy-per-tranche rule should
   suffice, but worth a documentation pass.
