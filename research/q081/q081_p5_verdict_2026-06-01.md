## Verdict A — RATIFIED. New SPEC: debit cash-budget cap + concurrent alert

**Status**: Per G-review 2 Q2/Q3/Q4 — ratified with calibration update.

### Specification

**Primary cap** (Q2 RATIFY-modified, Q3 RATIFY-60):
- `Σ debit_open ≤ 0.60 × current_liquid_cash`
- Denominator: **realtime** combined liquid cash (Schwab raw cash +
  Schwab cash-like + E-Trade raw cash), read at open-decision time
- **Dynamic percentage**, NOT a fixed dollar value (so cap adapts if PM
  rebalances)
- On violation: block new debit-strategy open; degrade to reduce_wait
- Cap floor: if liquid cash < $30k, BCD opens auto-blocked regardless
  of percentage math

**Concurrent-debit alert** (Q4 CHALLENGE-add):
- Alert (notification, not block) when Σ debit_open reaches 75% of
  current liquid cash
- This is the early warning for the forward-risk scenario reviewer
  flagged: future BCD frequency increase or hold-extension producing
  concurrent positions whose aggregate breaches cap
- Per memory `feedback_kill_gate_external_read`: false negatives need
  monitoring because "0 historical crowd-out" is a behavioral artifact
  of sequential ladder, not a structural guarantee

### Calibration: 60% (not 65%)

Per G2 Q3:
- 60% leaves headroom for future debit-strategy additions
- 65%→60% slack cost is small (~$1,850 cash, BCD shrinks proportionally)
- 60% is structural-not-documented (doesn't depend on future re-audit)

### Why % liquid (NOT % NLV)

Per G2 Q2: cap denominator MUST be the scarce resource it protects. In
cash-bound account that's liquid cash ($37k baseline), not NLV ($1.24M).
A 5% NLV cap = $62k > full liquid pool = effectively no cap.

## Verdict B — REVISED. Matrix routing: keep BCD with honest characterization

**Status**: Original "matrix unchanged because BCD has structural alpha"
framing is REFUTED by §F stratification. New characterization below
needs 2nd quant G-review 2 re-read.

### Original claim (now refuted)

BCD's +8pp mean dominance over QQQ = structural alpha justifying matrix
status quo.

### Refutation evidence

§F stratification:
- **UP windows (n=10)**: BCD +23.58% vs QQQ +4.20% → **all 10 wins**, +19.4pp
- **FLAT windows (n=2)**: BCD +3.35% vs QQQ +0.93% → 2/2 wins, +2.4pp
- **DOWN windows (n=9)**: BCD -7.52% vs QQQ -4.14% → only 2/9 wins, **-3.4pp**

The +8pp mean is fully explained by up-window concentration. BCD is
"asymmetric leveraged beta": ~3x QQQ amplification on the way up, ~1.8x
QQQ loss on the way down.

§G Sortino:
- BCD aggregate Sortino +1.454 vs QQQ +0.102 → looks favorable
- But within-DOWN-stratum: BCD Sortino -0.860 vs QQQ -0.878 → near
  identical in stress

BCD does NOT have catastrophically worse downside Sortino than QQQ. The
Sortino advantage is concentrated in non-stress regimes.

### Revised characterization

**BCD = regime-conditional leveraged-beta with vega cushion.**
- Up regime: dominates QQQ via short-leg theta + long-leg delta capture
- Flat regime: dominates QQQ via theta-only harvest
- Down regime: underperforms QQQ on mean by ~3.4pp; Sortino comparable

The matrix's BULLISH trend filter is the implicit directional gate.
Trend signal accuracy is imperfect (sample shows 10/21 = 48% up windows
vs random baseline ~50%), so BCD inherits down-windows.

### Two paths for Verdict B (decision for G2 re-read)

**B-1 (recommended): Keep BCD in matrix with honest characterization**

Rationale:
- Aggregate +8pp net edge across full 3y sample (mix of regimes)
- Cap+alert (Verdict A) governs single-trade cash risk
- Vega cushion in DOWN windows is real residual alpha (BCD Sortino in
  DOWN ≈ QQQ Sortino, not catastrophically worse)
- BULLISH trend filter is the de-facto directional gate
- Deferring matrix to Q082 adds delay with limited in-sample evidence

Trade-off accepted:
- BCD is regime-conditional, not unconditional. If future regime is
  systematically down-biased, BCD aggregate edge erodes
- This is structural risk PM accepts when using BCD in current matrix
- Cap+alert (Verdict A) is the prophylactic against the size dimension
  of the same risk

**B-2 (alternative): Defer matrix verdict to Q082**

Rationale:
- Original load-bearing claim ("structural alpha") refuted; remaining
  case for status quo is "still net positive in 3y" which is weaker
- Multi-regime / longer-sample analysis could test whether BCD aggregate
  edge holds across 2008/2020-style stress regimes
- Cap+alert (Verdict A) ships regardless

Trade-off accepted:
- Delay closure of Q081
- Q082 scope expansion (synthetic BCD reconstruction across regimes,
  per G-review 1 Q2 option B)

**1st quant lean**: B-1, but acknowledge Q082 follow-up is reasonable
if 2nd quant prefers more rigorous matrix verdict.
