## TL;DR

The right structural fix isn't shortening the IVP window — it's **adding a new matrix cell route for the 67.5% of NORMAL×BULL days currently sent to reduce_wait**. Route `NORMAL × IV_LOW × BULLISH → BCD` (instead of reduce_wait).

Evidence: BCD in this proposed cell outperforms Q082's already-validated `LOW_VOL × BULL → BCD` cell on win rate (73% vs 66%) and mean PnL ($1,410 vs $1,016), with similar Sortino (~0.8). The +vega structural cushion that makes BCD work in LOW_VOL is even more valuable in NORMAL × IV_LOW where vol expansion is more frequent (29.6% vs 24.3% of forward 21d windows see VIX +5vp).

The 67.5% iv_signal=LOW universe characterization:

- These are **regime transition** days, not "stable low vol"

The matrix correctly identifies these as "don't sell premium" but **incorrectly routes to reduce_wait** when the right response is "**buy premium via BCD**".

## 4. SPEC-113 design

### 4.1 Matrix change (single cell)

Catalog.py `CANONICAL_MATRIX`:

**Before**:
```python
"NORMAL": {
    "HIGH":    {"BULLISH": "bull_put_spread",    "NEUTRAL": "iron_condor",    "BEARISH": "iron_condor"},
    "NEUTRAL": {"BULLISH": "bull_put_spread",    "NEUTRAL": "iron_condor",    "BEARISH": "iron_condor"},
    "LOW":     {"BULLISH": "reduce_wait",        "NEUTRAL": "reduce_wait",    "BEARISH": "reduce_wait"},
}
```

**After**:
```python
"NORMAL": {
    "HIGH":    {"BULLISH": "bull_put_spread",     "NEUTRAL": "iron_condor",    "BEARISH": "iron_condor"},
    "NEUTRAL": {"BULLISH": "bull_put_spread",     "NEUTRAL": "iron_condor",    "BEARISH": "iron_condor"},
    "LOW":     {"BULLISH": "bull_call_diagonal",  "NEUTRAL": "reduce_wait",    "BEARISH": "reduce_wait"},  # <-- BULLISH only
}
```

**One change**: `NORMAL × LOW × BULLISH` from `reduce_wait` to `bull_call_diagonal`.

**NEUTRAL and BEARISH trends remain reduce_wait** — those don't have the +delta tailwind, and Q082 already showed BCD is regime-conditional leveraged-beta requiring bullish trend.

### 4.2 Gate interaction

BCD doesn't use the IVP_LOW gate (which is for premium-selling strategies). The relevant filter for BCD is the BCD-specific entry comfort filter (q078 ladder monitors `bull_call_diagonal: 56%`). This may also need updating to reflect the new cell context.

Per Q082 P10 findings, the existing BCD strategy is robust as long as:
- Matrix-cell + trend filter (BULLISH) ✓
- SPEC-111 cash cap ✓
- Sequential ladder enforcement ✓

No new gates needed for SPEC-113.

### 4.3 Cash budget implications

PM has ~$37k liquid cash (steady-state). BCD median debit ~$18-25k. Sequential ladder = one BCD at a time. With matrix change:
- BCD trade frequency: existing LOW_VOL ~6/year + new NORMAL × IV_LOW ~4/year = **~10/year**
- vs current PM experience: BCD only when VIX < 15 (LOW_VOL), rare in 2026 with VIX persistently 15+
- Effective trade count for PM: ~5-7x more BCD opportunities given current regime

### 4.4 Tail risk control

SPEC-111 cap at 60% liquid bounds single BCD exposure to ~$22k. Worst trade in P11 sim was -$9,975 — well within cap. P11 disaster rate (≤-$2,500 loss) was 0/82.

The "Q081 P4 vega tail asymmetry" concern was about BPS in low-IV — does NOT apply to BCD which is +vega. SPEC-113 introduces no new tail mechanism.

## 5. Caveats and risks

### 5.1 Skew bracket (Q082 P10 lesson, applies here too)

BCD synth uses BS-flat IV. Real chains have skew. For BCD (net +vega from long-leg dominance), Q082 P10 found:
- Skew steepening makes short-leg σ expand faster than long-leg σ
- Net vega gain in real chains < BS-flat synth shows
- Q082 estimated ~13% haircut on DOWN-window edge
- Aggregate edge haircut probably similar 10-15%

So real-chain expected mean PnL likely $1,200-1,260 (vs $1,410 synth). Still strongly positive.

### 5.2 Sample size

n=82 trades over 23 years. Block-bootstrap CI not yet computed but per Q082 P10 patterns, expected CI tight enough for action.

### 5.3 Regime overlap

Some "iv_signal=LOW" days are right after vol spike (vol just peaked, now declining). BCD's +vega is a tailwind when vol expands, a headwind when vol contracts. The 73% win rate suggests aggregate net positive but path-dependent.

If PM enters BCD after a fresh spike (post-peak, vol contracting), expected return is lower. Cannot avoid all such entries since sequential ladder doesn't predict forward direction. This is "regime-conditional leveraged beta" PM already accepted in Q082 B-1.

### 5.4 Doesn't address NORMAL × IV_NEUTRAL × BULL × IVP-out-of-band (state c, 23.6% of NORMAL×BULL)

Those days currently route reduce_wait due to IVP gate. SPEC-113 doesn't change that — only addresses iv_signal=LOW (67.5%). State (c) remains a separate question. Could explore in Q084 if PM wants further unlock.

### 5.5 Combined matrix BCD frequency

After SPEC-113, BCD routes for:
- LOW_VOL × BULL × any IVR (Q082 baseline)
- NORMAL × IV_LOW × BULL (new)

That's ~50% of all NORMAL+LOW_VOL days. Sequential ladder constraint = 1 BCD at a time. So total BCD exposure per year increases but stays bounded.
